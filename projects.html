Research Projects

Sperm Cell Motion and High-Throughput Separation in Microfluidic Devices (2021–Present)

Investigating sperm motility, rheotaxis, and boundary-following behavior in Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids that mimic the rheological properties of the female reproductive tract. The work aims to understand microscale sperm–fluid interactions and utilize these insights to develop high-throughput microfluidic platforms for selective sperm separation for assisted reproductive technologies such as IVF and ICSI. The research integrates microfluidic experiments with numerical simulations to analyze flow fields, shear rates, and swimmer dynamics.

Supervisor: Dr. Siddhartha Tripathi, BITS Pilani Goa Campus
Co-Supervisor: Dr. Vadiraj Hemadri, BITS Pilani Goa Campus

Microfluidic Isolation of White Blood Cells for Rare Disease Diagnostics (2024–Present)

Design and evaluation of microfluidic devices for label-free isolation of white blood cells for downstream analysis of rare diseases such as Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD). The work focuses on optimization of device geometry, flow control strategies, and experimental validation for point-of-care diagnostic applications.

Supervisor: Dr. Siddhartha Tripathi, BITS Pilani Goa Campus

Microfluidic Sperm Cell Sorting Device for Addressing Male Infertility (Oct 2021 – Apr 2022)

Developed a simple and efficient sealed T-shaped microfluidic device capable of separating progressively motile sperm cells in sufficient quantity for intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI). The study emphasized device simplicity, robustness, and practical applicability in clinical settings through experimental validation.

Supervisor: Dr. Siddhartha Tripathi, BITS Pilani Goa Campus

Performance Enhancement of Shell-and-Tube and Plate Heat Exchangers Using Nanofluids (2017–2019)

Experimental and numerical investigation of nanofluids as working fluids to enhance thermal performance of heat exchangers operating as evaporators. The study analyzed heat transfer enhancement, pressure drop characteristics, and overall system efficiency improvements.

Supervisor: Dr. Babaso Naik, Walchand College of Engineering, Sangli
