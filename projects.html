Research Projects

Sperm Cell Motion and High-Throughput Separation in Microfluidic Devices (2021–Present)

Investigation of sperm motility, rheotaxis, and boundary-following behaviour in Newtonian and non-Newtonian fluids mimicking physiological reproductive environments. This work aims to understand microscale sperm–fluid interactions and develop high-throughput microfluidic platforms for selective sperm separation for assisted reproductive technologies such as IVF and ICSI. The research integrates experimental microfluidics with numerical simulations to analyse flow fields, shear distributions, and swimmer dynamics.

Supervisor: Dr. Siddhartha Tripathi, BITS Pilani Goa Campus
Co-Supervisor: Dr. Vadiraj Hemadri, BITS Pilani Goa Campus

Microfluidic Isolation of White Blood Cells for Rare Disease Diagnostics (2024–Present)

Design and evaluation of label-free microfluidic devices for isolation of white blood cells for downstream rare disease diagnostics such as Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD). Focus areas include device geometry optimisation, controlled hydrodynamic flow conditions, and experimental validation for point-of-care biomedical applications.

Supervisor: Dr. Siddhartha Tripathi, BITS Pilani Goa Campus

Microfluidic Sperm Sorting Device for Addressing Male Infertility (Oct 2021 – Apr 2022)

Development and experimental validation of a sealed T-shaped microfluidic device for efficient separation of progressively motile sperm suitable for intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI). The study emphasised simplicity, robust design, and translational clinical applicability.

Supervisor: Dr. Siddhartha Tripathi, BITS Pilani Goa Campus

Performance Enhancement of Heat Exchangers Using Nanofluids (2017–2019)

Experimental and computational investigation of nanofluids to enhance thermal performance of shell-and-tube and plate heat exchangers operating as evaporators. The study analysed heat transfer enhancement mechanisms, pressure drop characteristics, and system efficiency improvements.

Supervisor: Dr. Babaso Naik, Walchand College of Engineering
